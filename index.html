Welcome, to The Wheel of Life

In Buddhism, The Wheel of Life represents samsara, the continuous cycle of birth, life, and death.

The Wheel of Life is the most influential piece of Bhuddist art. It tells the story of the journey to enlightenment.

In the center of the wheel are the three positions of ignorance. These positiong give rise to positive and negative actions that influence karma and how our karma affects our journey to enlightenment.

The outermost ring shows how the the three positions and karma produce a cyclical existence in more detail.

There are 10 areas in the wheel that are clickable. Can you find them all?

Yama

This is Yamantaka, The Lord of Death, devouring the wheel of samsara. He is not meant to be scary, in fact, he is there to help us understand our life's purpose as taught by Mayahana Buddhism!

Yama is holding up a giant circular mirror, but instead of showing the refection of the viewer, its reflection is a representation of all the past experiences, and all the possibilities open to us throughout our journey to Enlightenment.

Three Positions of Ignorance

In the center of the wheel of samsara are three creatures: a pig, a snake and bird.

  • The pig represents ignorance
  • The snake represents anger
  • The bird represents attachment

The three positions of ignorance influence Karma by representing the greed, hatered, and dillusion that plague human life.

They are shown connected in a circle in this depiction to represent that the three are intertwined with one another and without one, the entire wheel would crumble.

Karma

Karma is the cycle of cause and effect.

The light and dark sections of the ring of karma show that you can affect your karma both positively and negitively.

  • The lighter band shows happy people moving upward, to higher realms, as a result of positive actions
  • The darker band shows unhappy people moving downwards, into lower realms, as a result of negative actions

Our Karma influences our journey through the six realms of samsara. The circle represents our experience in cyclical existence through birth, life, and death. Each relm is full of its own sufferings that one must endure on the path to enlightenment.

The realm of the Gods

One is born into the God realm as a result of many positive actions in a previous life that positively influenced their karma.

It is not all good in the realm of the Gods however. While living joyful lives full of abundance and pleasure, they are exhausting their karma and will subsequently be born into a lower realm on a following rebirth.

The realm of Man

In the realm of Man, people constantly suffer the hardships of life, sickness, and death. They are subjected to hunger, thirst, attack from enemies, and seperation from friends and loved ones.

People often recieve what they least desire, while their greatest wishes slip away.

Despite these sufferings, the realm of Man is considered to be the best suited to dharma, which is learning the teachings of the Buddha. This is because they are not as heavily distracted by pleasure as they would be in the other higher realms, and they are not as prone to the sufferings of the lower three realms.

The realm of Animals

Those who have been born into the realm of animals live a life of constant fear.

Animals who live in the wild are under constant threat of attack by other animals and worldly threats.

Those who are under the domestication of man are often overworked and used for food.

The realm of Hell

The realm of hell is made up of 12 seperate hells, some hot and some cold. Each with their own sufferings.

When a person is born into the realm of hell, they are there to suffer the consequences for past actions done out of anger and hatred that negatively influenced their karma.

It is not all bad though! Rebirth into a higher realm is always possible after a stay in the realm of Hell.

The realm of Hungry Ghosts

Those born into the realm of Hungry Ghosts suffer from constant hunger and thirst.

They wander in constant search of food and drink. Even when they do find water or food, it often makes them sick becuse they have been without for so long.

The realm of the Demi-Gods

Like the realm of the Gods, the realm of the Demi-Gods offers abundance and pleasure. However, inhabitants of the realm of the Demi-Gods suffer from constant anger and fighting.

Those in the realm of the Demi-Gods are often wounded or killed as a result of fighting amongst themselves, and with the Gods.

Twelve Links of Dependant Origination

The Twelve Links of Origination show in more detail the cause and effect of karma and how it influences our lives.

The links can be thought of as influencing the entire journey through samsara, or show the effects of a single action in one lifetime.

Represented in the ring are:

  • lack of knowledge
  • willing constructive activities
  • conciousness
  • mental and physical existence
  • the six major senses
  • physical contact
  • pain
  • thirst
  • grasping
  • coming of age
  • being born
  • old age and death

By understanding the twelve links, we are better able to understand how our actions affect our reality.
